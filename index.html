About Me

My name is Ion Paschalidis. After graduating from the Ecole Nationale d'Ingénieurs de Brest (ENIB), a French engineering school, where I studied for a diploma in systems engineering, I decided to continue my studies and enrolled in the MSc in Naval Architecture offered by the KTH Royal Institute of Technology, more precisely in the Fluid Mechanics track.

I made this decision because I aspire to collaborate with researchers on multidisciplinary projects, providing them with advanced tools and technologies to support their work. For example, I am interested in designing energy-efficient underwater robots capable of operating in challenging marine environments. By doing so, I aim to contribute to the understanding and preservation of marine ecosystems, enabling researchers to monitor ocean health, track biodiversity, and address global environmental challenges more effectively. In summary, I hope to contribute to the development of sustainable technologies that support both industrial innovation and environmental conservation.
